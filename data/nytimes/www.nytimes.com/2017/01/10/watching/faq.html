What Is Watching, and How Do I Use the Site?

by Watching Staff

Jan. 10, 2017UPDATED Dec. 14, 2017

There are more movies and TV shows to watch — and more ways to watch them — than ever before.

But there are still only 24 hours in a day.

We’re here to help. Here on Watching, a TV and movie recommendation website from The New York Times, we will help you to choose programs and films you’ll love and to find where they live.

We also publish a thrice-weekly email newsletter that delivers recommendations straight to your inbox and a monthly email newsletter that keeps you updated on the latest TV series and movies available to stream.

The site is open to all readers with a free NYTimes.com account or a paid subscription.

Do you want to jump in right away?

Great! Take a look at some of our collections, like Convincing Female Friendships, or TV to Make You Scared to Turn the Lights Off, or Orphans Making It in the World.

At the top of the site, you can tell us what you’re in the mood to watch or which genre you’re interested in, and we’ll give you a batch of recommendations from across many streaming services. Click through to see where to stream them.

To save a title for future reference, click on the “Save to Watch” buttons accompanying Watching recommendations. This will add the title to your personal Watchlist. When you’re ready to watch that title, or if you are just curious to review what you’ve saved, click the “Your Watchlist” button at the top right corner on a desktop browser to view your Watchlist. On a mobile device, click the person icon at the top right of the screen and select “Your Watchlist.”

Click the heart icons to let us know you like a movie or a show, which will help us recommend more things you may want to watch. The more you use these buttons, the better the site will be able to offer recommendations that fit your tastes.

If you give us your streaming preferences, we’ll tailor our recommendations to the streaming services you use.

General

Can I view Watching on my smartphone or tablet?

Yes. Watching is a website that is optimized for mobile, tablet and desktop devices. Find it at NYTimes.com/watching.

Does it cost anything to use Watching?

No. Access to Watching requires a free NYTimes.com account or a paid subscription to The New York Times. Anyone with access to Watching can read our movie and TV recommendations and use the website’s features.

Visits to the Watching website do not count toward the monthly limits on New York Times articles. Please note that articles in the TV and Movie sections of NYTimes.com that are not part of Watching will count toward your monthly limit.

Is there a Watching mobile app I can download for my smartphone or tablet?

Watching is currently a website, which means you can access it with any device through your web browser. We do not offer a mobile app at this time.

Login and Website Access

How do I access Watching?

Simply visit NYTimes.com/watching. If you are already logged in to NYTimes.com, you can access the site immediately. If you are not logged in but have a NYTimes.com account, we’ll eventually prompt you to log in with your usual credentials. If you do not have an existing account, you’ll eventually be prompted to create a free one by entering an email address and password. Or simply create one first by going here.

Contact watchingcare@nytimes.com for any issues relating to accessing the site. We’ll be glad to help.

I have a NYTimes.com account and tried to reset my password but didn’t receive an email to complete the process. Can you help?

Sure. As a first step, please check your spam folder to make sure the email isn’t there. If it isn’t, contact us at watchingcare@nytimes.com, and we will help you complete the process.

Do my visits to the Watching website count toward the monthly limit for NY Times?

No. Pages on the Watching site (those whose web addresses begin with NYTimes.com/watching) do not count toward your monthly limit. We will, however, occasionally link to other New York Times pages that count toward that limit.

Saving TV Shows and Movies for Later

How do I save a movie or TV title for later?

When navigating the website, you can save the titles you want to watch later by clicking on the ribbon icon or the “Save to Watch” button. Once clicked, the ribbon will change to purple to indicate that the title has been saved. The “Save to Watch” button will also change colors.

Once I save those titles, where can I find them?

The titles you save can all be found in your personal Watchlist. On desktop and laptop computers, you can easily navigate to your Watchlist by clicking on the “Your Watchlist” button at the top right of the site. On mobile devices you can click on the person icon at the top right of your screen and then click “Your Watchlist” in the dropdown menu.

In the Watchlist you will find all the titles you have saved, with the most recent at the top.

The Watchlist is accessible through the Watching website. It is not accessible directly through the New York Times mobile application.

Personalized TV and Movie Recommendations

Can I track the titles I have seen and liked?

Yes. You can mark titles you have seen and liked by clicking on the heart-shaped icons you’ll see across the site. The heart icon is typically positioned next to the ribbon icon or the “Save to Watch” button accompanying a title.

Liking a title will add it to the Liked section of your personal Watchlist. Once liked, you can unlike a title wherever you encounter it on the website by clicking again on the heart icon. This will remove it from the Liked section in your Watchlist.

Marking a title as liked will not add or remove titles that are in the “Saved to Watch” section of your Watchlist.

The Watching team will use the titles you have liked to help personalize our recommendations to you.

How do I find titles that are similar to the ones I’ve seen and liked?

The Watching team will use the titles you have liked to personalize our recommendations to you. Each time we have new personalized recommendations for you, a red dot will appear next to the “Your Watchlist” button in the top right portion of your screen on desktop computers, or next to the person icon at the top right of your screen on mobile devices. Clicking on the button will bring you to your Watchlist, where you’ll find a “For You” section with a “NEW” label next to it. In the “For You” section, you will find titles recommended by the Watching editorial team based on your likes.

As you continue liking titles across the site, more personalized recommendations will be added to the top the Liked section in your Watchlist.

To ensure you don’t forget a recommendation that interests you, save it for future reference by clicking on the ribbon icon.

Managing your Watchlist

How do I keep track of the titles I have watched?

When a title is in the Saved to Watch or Liked sections of your Watchlist, you can click on the ellipsis accompanying the title and then the “Mark as Watched” button. This will move the title to the Watched section of your Watchlist. You cannot mark a title as “watched” when it is not in one of those two Watchlist sections.

All titles marked as “watched” will display a checkmark at the top right of the image accompanying a title, wherever it is displayed on the website.

When a title is in the Watched section of your Watchlist, you can move it back to the Saved to Watch section by clicking on the ribbon icon or on the ellipsis and then on the “Save to Watch” button.

How do I remove a title from my Watchlist?

Remove a title from the “Saved to Watch” section of your Watchlist by clicking the purple ribbon icon. You will be able to re-save the title if you come across it on the site again. This will not remove it from the Liked section or the “For You” section of your Watchlist.

To remove a title from the Liked section, click on the purple heart icon.

To remove a title from the Saved to Watch, Liked and Watched sections of your Watchlist, click on the ellipsis accompanying the title and then on the “Delete from Watchlist” button.

Titles from the For You section cannot be marked as “watched” or deleted. As more personalized recommendations are added, however, older recommendations will be pushed down the list.

Content

How does Watching differ from the TV and Movie sections of The New York Times?

Watching is a recommendation site designed to help you navigate the increasingly overwhelming world of streaming movies and TV. The TV and Movie sections of NYTimes.com publish features, interviews, news and reviews.

When you find a TV show or a movie that looks interesting on Watching, get more information by heading to its recommendation page, where you can find:

• Information on where to stream the title and the ability to start watching with one click

• Guidance on why you should watch it (or why it may not be for you)

• Links to some of the best essays, interviews, recaps and reviews about the title from across the web

How often is Watching updated?

We publish new TV and movie recommendations and other types of related content every day.

I found an error in the TV show or movie pages. What do I do?

We welcome comments and suggestions, as well as notifications about errors that warrant correction. Contact us at watchingcare@nytimes.com.
